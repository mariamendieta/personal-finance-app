04 — Design Elements

Small details, big identity

Alebrije Accent Bar

A thin gradient strip using all accent colors. Used at page tops, slide edges, or as section dividers. The signature element.

Tropical Leaf Motifs

Hand-drawn leaf shapes at low opacity as background textures, or as small decorative elements in slide corners.

Alebrije Dot Pattern

Small colored dots inspired by alebrije painting. Used as decorative punctuation — never overwhelming, always intentional.

Ave del Paraíso Motif

A stylized bird of paradise flower — geometric, architectural, bold. The brand's structured counterpart to the organic leaf. Used as a brand mark or accent detail.

06 — Brand Rules

The guardrails

Do

  • Use Latin accents as small punctuations — a dot, a bar, a corner leaf
  • Let warm neutrals carry the visual weight
  • Mix organic hand-drawn elements with clean data
  • Keep layouts editorial and asymmetric
  • Use generous white space — let things breathe
  • Name accent colors in Spanish — it's part of the identity

Don't

  • Turn it into a fiesta — accents are spice, not the dish
  • Use all accent colors at once on a single slide
  • Default to "safe beige" — this brand has soul
  • Use stock illustrations or generic tech graphics
  • Center everything — asymmetry is the design language
  • Make it look like a wellness or yoga brand
