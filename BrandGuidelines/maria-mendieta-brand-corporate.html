04 — Design Elements · Corporate

Restrained, functional, distinctive

Corporate Accent Strip

Simplified to 3 tones: azul → verde hoja → verde claro. Cleaner, more corporate. Used at slide tops and section breaks.

Botanical Motifs

Title and closing slides only. Smaller, lower opacity. The ave del paraíso becomes a subtle signature, not a pattern. No leaves on data slides.

Data Visualization Palette

Primary: Azul Profundo + Verde Hoja. Secondary: Verde Claro. Alert/emphasis only: Coral Vivo, Marigold. No magenta in corporate charts.

06 — Brand Rules · Corporate

The guardrails

Do

  • Lead with Azul Profundo — it signals authority and trust
  • Use pure white or cool gray backgrounds — no cream
  • Keep botanicals to title + closing slides only
  • Let data and structure carry the visual weight
  • Use the 3-tone accent strip, not the full alebrije gradient
  • Match the workshop brand's fonts — consistency builds recognition

Don't

  • Use warm white or linen backgrounds — too soft for this context
  • Place botanical motifs on data or analysis slides
  • Use magenta in corporate mode — reserve for workshop
  • Over-decorate — every element should be functional
  • Lose the personality entirely — the serif fonts and accent strip keep you distinctive
  • Use the full alebrije dot pattern — too playful for boardrooms
